the last letter

Author: Threads.

Carlos found the folded letter inside an old book in his grandfather's library. The handwriting was elegant and feminine.

“Dear Miguel: I am writing you this last letter knowing that you will never read it. Tomorrow I am marrying another man, but my heart will always be yours. Always, Elena.”

The date: June 15, 1965. Sixty years ago.

Intrigued, Carlos investigated. He discovered that Elena was still alive, a widow, in a nearby nursing home. He took the letter, nervous about disturbing her peace.

As she read it, her wrinkled hands trembled, but she smiled. “Miguel did read it,” she said softly. “He was the photographer at my wedding. We met years later. We had a wonderful life in secret.”

Carlos, moved, asked, “Why did you keep it a secret?”

“True love sometimes needs silence to flourish,” she replied.

On his way out, Carlos bumped into a woman who was picking up fallen books. Her eyes were green, intense. “I'm sorry,” he said, helping her up.

She smiled. “Don't worry. Sometimes the best encounters begin with a stumble.”

And he knew, in that moment, that his own love story had just begun.

THE SEVENTH WHISPER OF THE FOREST

Author: Threads.

Elaine crossed the mossy stone boundary knowing she shouldn't. The Whispering Forest forbade mortals from entering, but her little brother had disappeared there.

Every ten steps, the wind carried a voice:
First whisper: “Turn back...”
Second: “You don't belong here...”

At the sixth whisper, she saw the figure: a being made of branches and moonlight, with eyes like deep pools. “Humans steal our magic,” said the creature.

“I'm only looking for my brother,” pleaded Elaine, showing a blue ribbon the boy wore.

The forest being bowed. "You lie with a pure heart. Your brother is safe. I followed you to protect him... from you."

Seventh whisper: “You are the threat...”

A sharp pain in her head. Memories flooded her mind: her, angry, wishing her brother would disappear, and her rage unleashing ancient magic that sent him into the forest.

“Your anger betrays you,” said the creature. “Control your emotions or you will lose him forever.”

Elaine took a deep breath and calmed her heart. As she did so, the forest glowed and her brother appeared, asleep under a tree.

She learned that night that some forests do not hide monsters... but mirrors of our own soul

THE PENDULUM CLOCK ROOM

Author: Theads.

Aunt Abigail's inheritance came with one condition: spend a full night in her Victorian mansion. Daniel laughed when he read it, until the lawyer added, “No one has ever managed to stay past 3:17 AM.”

At 10 PM, Daniel entered the house. Dust, darkness, and silence. Everything was normal until 1:47 a.m., when the large pendulum clock in the hall stopped.

Tick... tock... silence.

From then on, he felt like he was being watched. In the hallway mirror, reflections that weren't his. Whispers saying, “Leave...”

At 3:17 a.m., a door opened by itself: the clock room. Inside, he found not mechanisms, but diaries. Dozens of them. All belonging to Aunt Abigail, with the same date: the night of her death, but written as if she were still alive.

“Today Daniel came. I hope he can see me...”

A voice behind him: “They never look for me enough.”

Daniel turned around. There was Abigail, transparent and smiling. “I'm not trapped here by a ghost, Daniel. I'm trapped by oblivion. The family let me die alone.”

The clock started ticking again. Daniel understood: some mysteries don't need to be solved... just accompanied. And he sat down to listen to her story until dawn.
